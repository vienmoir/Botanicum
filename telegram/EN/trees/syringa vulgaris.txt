Syringa vulgaris (lilac or common lilac) is a species of flowering plant in the olive family Oleaceae, native to the Balkan Peninsula, where it grows on rocky hills.
It is a large deciduous shrub or multistemmed small tree, growing to 6–7 m high, producing secondary shoots ("suckers") from the base or roots, with stem diameters up to 20 cm
